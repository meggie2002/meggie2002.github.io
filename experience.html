Data Science Intern

Teal Climate

Fall 2025 | Calgary, AB

Challenge

Teal Climate needed efficient, scientifically valid geospatial workflows for carbon stock assessment in nature-based ecosystems. The manual processing of drone imagery took hours per site, and satellite imagery lacked sufficient resolution for accurate tree detection.

Approach

I built end-to-end preprocessing pipelines using OpenDroneMap, developed computational optimization strategies for large-scale data processing, and implemented super-resolution models through transfer learning to enhance satellite imagery quality. Critical to success was validating all outputs against ecological literature to ensure scientific accuracy.

Impact & Learning

  • Created reproducible geospatial workflows reducing preprocessing time and improving data quality
  • Developed quality control procedures that caught calculation errors through domain validation
  • Learned that real-world data science prioritizes data quality and scientific rigor over algorithmic sophistication
  • Gained expertise in geospatial tools (OpenDroneMap, QGIS, ArcGIS) and remote sensing techniques
