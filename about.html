My Story

I'm graduating from the University of Calgary in January 2026 with a degree in Data Science, but my journey has been about more than just learning algorithms and models. Through internships at Teal Climate and Proactive Seniors, I discovered something important: the most valuable data scientists aren't just technically skilled they translate complex capabilities into solutions that work in the real world.

At Teal Climate, I learned that building preprocessing pipelines for drone imagery wasn't just about code it was about ensuring every output could be trusted for real environmental decisions. When I caught a critical calculation error through scientific literature validation, I realized that computational correctness and scientific validity are two different things. Both matter.

Working with Proactive Seniors taught me a different lesson. Kathy needed AI to help serve more families, but what she really needed was someone who could evaluate platforms against her actual constraints: Canadian data residency, ease of use for non-technical staff, and protecting sensitive health information. The solution wasn't about finding the most powerful AI it was about finding what would actually work for her business.

These experiences shaped how I approach data science. I don't just ask "What's the best model?" I ask "What problem are we really solving?" and "Will this work in practice?" I've learned to balance technical rigor with practical constraints, to communicate across audiences, and to validate outputs not just computationally but within their domain context.

I'm drawn to work that has real impact climate tech, healthcare AI, conservation analytics. But more than any specific domain, I'm motivated by the challenge of figuring out what actually works and making it happen.
